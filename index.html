I'm Carlie and I graduated this May from Mizzou with a degree in data journalism. I've previously worked at The Dallas Morning News, Investigative Reporters & Editors, The Columbia Missourian and The Shreveport Times.


Whether it’s writing scrapers, analyzing data, designing graphics or developing apps - I want to combine journalism and technology to tell stories in innovative, captivating and impactful ways.


My biggest goal is not just to inform, but to encourage the public to want to stay informed. I also enjoy improving newsroom technologies to make it as easy as possible for journalists to do their job.


Below are some projects I've developed and designed over the past few years:


Reynolds Journalism Institute

RJI Alexa Skill (source code)

I spent the Fall 2017 semester working as the developer alongside two journalism students for an Alexa Skill for the Reynolds Journalism Institute. Our skill scrapes articles from their RSS feed based on tags said by the user. I wrote the skill itself in Node.js and implemented it with a Lambda function, and I wrote the scraper in Python. The scraper stores the data in a DynamoDB table which updates via an additional Lambda function. I created a how-to guide on how to do the skill here.


The Dallas Morning News

Restaurant Inspections (source code)

I helped develop a Django app from back-end to front-end that tracks restaurant health inspections across Dallas and Tarrant counties. I wrote several Python scrapers to scrape the data and store the data in a PostgreSQL database. Here's an article explaining how it works and why we made it.


Maps (source code)

I made several maps with Mapbox and a script that connects Google Sheets to an Amazon S3 bucket so the reporter could edit the data. My favorite is this one that shows the history of skyscrapers in downtown Dallas.


Divine Nine embeddable (source code)

For a story about the Dallas Divine Nine, I worked with a reporter to develop an embeddable that showed facts about each chapter.


Book slider (source code)

I teamed up with another intern to develop this project that tests reader's knowledge of summer reading books that North Texas schools were assigning. I used a jQuery plugin to make a series of image sliders where the top image was a synopsis of the book and the image underneath revealed the book cover, title and author.

The Columbia Missourian

2016 election map (source code)

For this map, I worked on a team to build a map of Missouri that displayed results from the U.S. presidential and senate races as they came in. I built the map with TopoJSON files and used D3 and Underscore.js to connect the data to it.

Special sections (full list)

Leadership Culture (source code)

Most of the special sections I made were long form pieces. One example was this piece on leadership culture at Mizzou.


A Historic Fall at MU (source code)

I used Timeline JS to design an interactive timeline of events leading up to the fall 2015 protests at Mizzou. The timeline received over 200,000 views within the month and was linked to by multiple national outlets including The New York Times. This piece also won first place in multimedia package at the Missouri College Media Association in April 2016 and is prominently featured on the Timeline JS website as an example.


Explaining Title IX (source code)

I worked alongside a reporter to make a Vox cards-esque interactive slider to explain what Title IX is. This won an honorable mention for multimedia package from the Missouri College Media Association in April 2016.


Mizzou in Review 2014-15 (source code)
